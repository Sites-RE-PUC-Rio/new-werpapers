Towards an Ontology of Goal-Oriented Requirements

Pedro Pignaton Negri; Vítor E. Silva Souza; André Luiz de Castro Leal; Ricardo de Almeida Falbo; Giancarlo Guizzardi

Workshop on Requirements Engineering (WER)2017

Leer PDFVer ediciónGoogle Scholar

Resumen

Goal-Oriented Requirements Engineering (GORE) has grown into an important area of research in the past decades. Still, some of its corners remain dark, since diferent GORE languages do not provide a well-founded conceptualization of the domain and are not consensual. This may lead to ambiguous or weak understanding of GORE concepts. In this paper, we introduce the Goal-Oriented Requirements Ontology (GORO), a domain ontology founded on the Uni ed Foundational Ontol- ogy (UFO) that intends to represent the nature and relations of concepts surrounding the GORE domain.We use GORO to explore and clarify the semantics used, sometimes implicitly, by well-known GORE languages.
